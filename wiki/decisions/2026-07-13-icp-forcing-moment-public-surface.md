# Decision - Lead With Knowledge Continuity Under A Forcing Moment

Date: 2026-07-13
Status: accepted for the current public and planning baseline

## Context

The owner ICP brief and current market review indicate that generic PRD generation is inexpensive and weakly differentiated. ArchFlow's stronger system is the maintained, source-grounded company brain plus bounded agent execution, verification, memory promotion, and lifecycle control.

## Decision

- Lead the current first-cohort hypothesis with enterprise-readiness, onboarding/hiring, and key-person-departure forcing moments.
- Describe PRD, ICP, content, and outreach as generated execution architectures inside the knowledge-continuity system.
- Use a seven-layer buyer story on the public website and an eight-route documentation/operator dashboard for exact system state.
- Keep the modeled ROI scenario at a zero recovery default and label it as heuristic until an observed baseline exists.
- Put installable repository/MCP/admin productization behind E8.0, which depends on E5 market evidence and E7 safety proof.
- Keep provider execution disabled until durable single-use approval and spend enforcement exist.

## Consequences

- Active workflows, content templates, role packs, retrieval authority, and task contracts use the current cohort and forcing-moment model.
- Historical PRD-first/50-500 material remains available as superseded history but receives a retrieval penalty.
- The website and dashboard can be tested locally without implying production freshness or validated demand.
- E5.1 primary evidence is the next business-validation lane; E8 implementation is not the next step.
