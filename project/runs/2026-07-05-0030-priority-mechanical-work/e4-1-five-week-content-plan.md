# E4.1 Five-Week Content Plan

Date: 2026-07-05
Status: draft for AF Review
Audience: B2B SaaS product leaders
Offer frame: Product Discovery-to-Production PRD Pack
Publication status: not approved

## Purpose

This draft turns the current ArchFlow proof state into a five-week content plan.
It is a planning artifact only. It does not publish content, schedule content,
edit profiles, or claim validated demand.

## Content Rules

- Lead with category education and proof, not founder-build narration.
- Use ArchFlow company voice by default.
- Label evidence as FACT, INTERPRETATION, HYPOTHESIS, or GAP.
- Use artifact-backed claims from public-safe run folders.
- Treat buyer pain and ICP fit as HYPOTHESIS until E2/E6/E7 evidence upgrades
  them.
- Do not claim customers, revenue, ROI, production impact, or validated demand.

## Week 1 Draft Brief

Working title: The first PRD gate is source boundary, not generation.

FACT:
- ArchFlow has a public-safe KB update principle and a source/API/data-model
  packet.
- The current evidence boundary separates allowed public summaries, gated tools,
  and blocked private material.

INTERPRETATION:
- Product leaders do not only need faster PRD drafting. They need a way to know
  what evidence is allowed, what is redacted, and what remains uncertain before
  engineering starts.

HYPOTHESIS:
- B2B SaaS product teams with repeated discovery-to-delivery handoff work will
  value a reviewed PRD pack if it preserves source lineage and unresolved gaps.

GAP:
- This has not yet been validated by paid diagnostics, source-packet
  willingness, proposal requests, or budget-owner conversations.

Suggested CTA:
- "Where does your team lose the most source context before a PRD reaches
  engineering?"

## Week 2 Draft Brief

Working title: A meeting summary is not a production handoff.

FACT:
- The current method separates context digest, PRD, acceptance criteria, decision
  log, and KB handoff as distinct outputs.

INTERPRETATION:
- A useful PRD workflow should survive readback by a future operator, not only
  produce a polished document.

HYPOTHESIS:
- Teams that already use meetings, research notes, and customer calls may need
  stronger handoff structure more than another note-taking layer.

GAP:
- E2 account evidence and E6 outreach have not yet proven this as buyer demand.

## Week 3 Draft Brief

Working title: Every PRD claim needs a label.

FACT:
- The E2 research route requires evidence grades and downgrades social-only
  signals to HYPOTHESIS.

INTERPRETATION:
- Evidence labels reduce the chance that assumptions enter the backlog as if
  they were verified requirements.

HYPOTHESIS:
- Product teams with fast-growing PM groups may feel this pain most when
  customer research, product ops, and delivery handoff overlap.

GAP:
- Real account cards and role verification are not collected in this run.

## Week 4 Draft Brief

Working title: The review gate is where AI work becomes usable.

FACT:
- Public templates now require public-safety, evidence labels, and owner
  approval before publication.

INTERPRETATION:
- The review gate is not bureaucracy; it is the point where unsupported claims
  are downgraded before they influence product or market decisions.

HYPOTHESIS:
- This gate is a useful sales differentiator against generic AI drafting tools.

GAP:
- Competitor and buyer evidence still need E2/E6 support.

## Week 5 Draft Brief

Working title: The first offer is a paid diagnostic, not a vague AI automation pitch.

FACT:
- The project plan defines validated demand as buyer risk: paid diagnostic,
  prepayment, approved paid start, firm paid intent, or source-packet plus
  scope/timeline/budget-owner path.

INTERPRETATION:
- Content should route toward source-packet willingness and concrete diagnostic
  conversations, not likes or generic compliments.

HYPOTHESIS:
- A Product Discovery-to-Production PRD Pack is easier to test when the CTA asks
  for a real workflow bottleneck and source boundary.

GAP:
- No E6/E7 demand proof exists yet.

## Review Checklist

- AF Review verifies public safety and claim strength.
- Owner chooses profile CTA and link target before publication.
- E3 diagnostic readiness is checked before routing traffic to a form.
- E5 metrics are ready before publication volume increases.
- No content is posted until owner approval is recorded.
