# Context Digest

## FACT

- The current company agenda is to build the first proof on internal material before making customer-facing claims.
- The working conversion path is: dialogue or meeting input, structured context, PRD, responsibility matrix, agent-ready knowledge base, review gate, then later ICP and market work.
- The two-sided discussion contains one side pushing for a structured product and automation engine, and another side pressing on audience clarity, content positioning, website structure, and demand validation.
- The source asks for parallel analysis by topic first, then synthesis, then task definition.
- Speaker labels in the transcript are not reliable enough for hard attribution in public files.

## INTERPRETATION

- The first side is best represented as the builder/operator position: define the system, build the knowledge base, make the workflow repeatable, and turn conversations into execution artifacts.
- The second side is best represented as the market/content position: identify who the first customer is, clarify the offer, create content tests, and avoid claiming demand before evidence exists.
- These positions are complementary. The company should not choose one over the other; it should use the builder position to create the proof workflow and the market position to shape what the workflow researches and validates next.
- E1.2 should be the first live proof cycle, not a broad research sprint.

## HYPOTHESIS

- The most useful first product proof is a dialogue-to-PRD and agent-task engine for teams that need structured execution from messy meeting material.
- A second likely product lane is a market-research and competitor-analysis report engine, but it needs separate validation.
- The website should present modular solution blocks and test which block attracts the strongest response.
- The future dashboard should be an operator control panel after the proof workflow is stable, not the primary knowledge system.

## GAP

- Customer segment evidence is not yet validated.
- The source does not provide reliable speaker-by-speaker ownership.
- No paid intent, firm demand, or conversion benchmark has been proved yet.
- Market research and web research belong in the next layer after the first internal proof run.
- Obsidian/Nexus live mirroring is still a later step unless the vault target and schema are explicitly selected.

## Method

The analysis separates facts, interpretations, hypotheses, and gaps. It keeps role attribution inferred, stores only public-safe synthesis, and routes future research into E2 rather than mixing it into E1.2 proof execution.
