# ICP Knowledge Node - Product Knowledge Continuity

Date: 2026-07-13
Status: reviewed hypothesis baseline; not validated demand
Primary source ID: `SRC-ICP-20260713` - owner-supplied ICP brief, summarized without copying raw private material

## Decision summary

ArchFlow should lead with a forcing moment that exposes product-knowledge risk. It should not lead with generic AI automation or PRD-writing speed.

The narrow first cohort is a hypothesis:

- horizontal, product-led B2B SaaS;
- roughly 30-75 employees;
- US/EU and English-working teams;
- Series A to early Series B as a directional stage proxy;
- product knowledge distributed across people, workspace tools, and code;
- an active deadline or continuity risk that makes the cost visible.

The likely buyer is Head of Product, VP Product, or CPO. A senior PM is usually the champion; below roughly 40 employees, a founder may buy. CTO or engineering leadership is secondary. The economic owner must be verified for each forcing moment.

In this node, PM means Product Manager. Project Manager and PMO are title-confusion exclusions, not assumed buyers. Product Ops may be a user, operator, or champion for chronic retrieval, but is not the default economic buyer without account evidence.

## Evidence labels

FACT:

- The owner brief ranks enterprise questionnaire/RFP and onboarding/hiring as the two strongest forcing moments.
- Key-person departure is acute but difficult to schedule as an acquisition cohort.
- AI-agent context and repeated information search are plausible pains but weaker buying moments.
- PRD/spec writing is a downstream artifact, not a strong standalone wedge.
- The brief names Notion/Confluence, Linear/Jira, Slack, and GitHub as common source surfaces.
- The brief names recent funding, open product/engineering roles, a senior-product departure, and enterprise/SOC 2 motion as public research signals. A signal is not a confirmed forcing moment.
- The current public product can demonstrate architecture, service packaging, a diagnostic model, operator documentation, a browser-local editor, and guarded API contracts.

INTERPRETATION:

- The product category is a knowledge-continuity operating system for product teams.
- The first service is a bounded knowledge-reliability setup; the longer-term product is an installable administration, retrieval, agent-loop, and proof layer.
- Every outward message should connect a forcing moment to one reviewable artifact and one safe next action.

HYPOTHESIS:

- The 30-75-person band is more responsive than adjacent bands.
- Product leadership owns the buying decision for each trigger.
- Buyers will grant a bounded external operator access to source systems.
- They will pay for maintained knowledge continuity after the initial setup.
- Warm introductions, manually verified LinkedIn research, and narrow email are the best initial channel sequence.
- API-documentation visual language increases trust and comprehension for this audience.

GAP:

- The 80-100 employee range is unresolved.
- RFP pain may be owned by Security, Sales Engineering, or RevOps rather than Product.
- Preferred channel, content format, website style, buying committee, budget, price, and access tolerance lack primary interview evidence.
- Quantitative pain and ROI statements in the source brief do not include a complete embedded bibliography and cannot be reused as facts.

## Observable-signal routing

| Public signal | What it may indicate | Verification needed before messaging |
|---|---|---|
| Recent raise | Hiring, onboarding, process change, or enterprise expansion may follow | Current hiring plan, product-team change, and actual knowledge-continuity pain |
| Open PM or engineering roles | Team expansion or replacement may create decision-recovery work | Role freshness, hiring owner, onboarding volume, and affected knowledge surfaces |
| Senior product departure | An acute continuity or ownership gap may exist | Departure recency, transition status, affected decisions, and current accountable owner |
| Enterprise or SOC 2 motion | Questionnaire, due-diligence, or evidence-readiness work may be active | Deadline, answer owner, Product/Security/Sales Engineering/RevOps routing, and access boundary |

These signals qualify research. They do not authorize contact, prove pain, or establish demand.

## Anti-ICP and deferral rules

Defer the first cohort when the account is:

- below 20 people with little durable product knowledge;
- roughly 100-150 people until the 80-100 transition is studied;
- an agency or services company rather than a product-led SaaS business;
- in a regulated vertical requiring a separate compliance architecture;
- already staffed with a mature knowledge/product-operations function;
- presenting a Project Manager or PMO title as if it were the Product leadership buyer;
- asking for autonomous outbound, unreviewed writeback, or a guaranteed ROI claim.

An anti-ICP result is a valid research outcome. Do not force-fit it into a sales task.

## Tool topology, not preference proof

The source brief names these operating surfaces; their presence must be verified per account:

- knowledge: Notion or Confluence;
- planning: Linear or Jira;
- coordination: Slack;
- implementation: GitHub;
- optional evidence: CRM, support, analytics, security, and sales-engineering systems.

ArchFlow should connect through bounded discovery, stable source IDs, permissions, freshness, provenance, and read-only defaults. A tool name never grants authority to crawl or write.

## Content hierarchy

1. Enterprise answer readiness: how to preserve evidence, ownership, and freshness before a deadline.
2. Onboarding continuity: how new people recover decisions without consuming the senior team.
3. Departure continuity: what to capture before critical product context leaves.
4. Decision systems for AI: why faster generation without governed context can accelerate inconsistency.
5. Knowledge-system diagnostics: source map, owner map, freshness, contradiction, and retrieval proof.
6. PRD/backlog output: proof that the maintained spine can produce useful artifacts, not the lead category.

Recommended first formats are checklists, annotated architecture diagrams, teardown articles, small benchmark reports, and operator walkthroughs. Format preference remains a test, not a fact.

## Visual and documentation hypothesis

Use a calm technical system, not generic AI spectacle:

- one strong architectural metaphor;
- progressive disclosure from outcome to implementation;
- API-documentation patterns: stable navigation, search, concept guides, reference, examples, status, and explicit failure states;
- evidence and hypothesis labels that are visually distinct;
- responsive diagrams with static and reduced-motion fallbacks;
- formulas and runtime boundaries visible near claims;
- dark technical operating surfaces paired with readable light documentation surfaces.

The current benchmark set includes OpenAI Developers, Anthropic Platform Docs, GitHub API Docs, Stripe API Reference, Vercel Docs, Cloudflare Developers, Supabase Docs, Palantir Foundry Docs, and Legora. These are design references selected by the owner and architecture review, not evidence of buyer preference.

## Audience evidence update hook

When a new interview, account signal, message result, or payment event appears:

1. Create or update a dated evidence card with source, role, account, trigger, freshness, consent, and public/private boundary.
2. Label each claim FACT, INTERPRETATION, HYPOTHESIS, or GAP.
3. Map the evidence to one trigger segment and one role route.
4. Keep warm, cold, inbound, and content cohorts separate.
5. Send any outward claim, contact, price, access request, or publication through AF Review and owner approval.
6. Promote only reviewed conclusions into this node; preserve contradictory evidence and supersession history.
7. Refresh Graphify/dashboard indexes after file changes; do not make generated graph labels the canonical conclusion.

## Decision metrics

Track stage changes rather than vanity totals:

- two-signal account verification;
- current-role and trigger verification;
- interview completion;
- outsider-access willingness and allowed scope;
- response and objection by forcing moment;
- source-packet or diagnostic commitment;
- proposal and paid-intent state;
- payment or approved paid start;
- claim-source completeness and reviewer verdict.

Attention, directory size, market-size arithmetic, internal proof, or a positive comment is not validated demand.
