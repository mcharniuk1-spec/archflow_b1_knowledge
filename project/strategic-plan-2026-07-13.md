# ArchFlow Strategic Plan - Knowledge Architecture First

Status: active planning and implementation baseline as of 2026-07-13. The public task-contract index is [`task-contract-index-2026-07-13.md`](task-contract-index-2026-07-13.md); the external task board is a coordination mirror, not the only reconstruction source.

## Strategy reset

ArchFlow is not primarily a PRD generator. The July market package shows that first-draft PRD generation is inexpensive and widely available. The defensible system is the maintained company brain: source boundaries, provenance, cross-feature consistency, goal-to-execution continuity, review, safe memory promotion, and measurable reliability.

The ICP review narrows the first market hypothesis to roughly 30-75-person product-led B2B SaaS under an active forcing moment. Enterprise questionnaire/RFP, onboarding, and key-person departure lead; AI-agent context remains experimental; PRD creation is a downstream artifact.

The plan now has eight epics. Every active task separates workflow status from evidence state.

## Required task contract

Every renewed task states:

- legacy ID when migrated;
- evidence state: Proven, Review, Planned, Blocked, or Superseded;
- objective and dependency IDs;
- accountable owner role and independent reviewer role;
- expected artifact and objective acceptance criteria;
- exact proof gate;
- risk and approval boundary;
- one next executable action and an in-page checklist.

## Renewed epic spine

### E1 - Knowledge Architecture And Governance

Objective: establish authoritative knowledge layers, provenance, promotion, reconciliation, and drift control.

Task families:

1. Reconcile the proof ledger and legacy task lineage.
2. Maintain authority, corpus, privacy, provenance, retention, and supersession policies.
3. Keep WikiLLM canonical and Obsidian human-readable without competing memories.
4. Maintain decision, issue, run, insight, and memory-promotion lifecycles.

Completion gate: a future operator can recover current mission, authority, proof, gaps, and next action from the approved memory layer with source traceability and no private/public boundary failure.

### E2 - Loop Engineering And Goal Contracts

Objective: convert one business outcome into a bounded, resumable, independently verifiable goal and execution loop.

Task families:

1. Adopt the Goal Engineering state contract at G1.
2. Keep Loop Engineering at L1 and prove a provider-disabled L2 fixture.
3. Make LangGraph the state and routing owner.
4. Prove attempts, budgets, stop/kill rules, checkpoints, maker/checker separation, and recovery.

Public lineage contracts are E2.1 goal/evidence schema, E2.2 bounded loop controller, E2.3 goal-to-task factory with the ICP golden flow, and E2.4 the evidence-backed ICP decision-contract fixture. Exact dependencies and proof gates are in the public task-contract index.

Completion gate: one goal runs from ready state to verified artifact or accurate blocked state without provider calls or unapproved side effects.

### E3 - Safe Tools, Skills, And Agent Architecture Factory

Objective: generate role architectures and adopt tools only when they are safe and measurably useful.

Task families:

1. Generate dependency graphs, role/skill packs, retrieval profiles, state machines, verification, memory, and benchmark contracts.
2. Prove Terra sole-writer integration with bounded Luna read-only branches.
3. Maintain adopt/trial/defer/reject decisions and versioned skill cards.
4. Require provenance, license, security scan, version pin, permission review, rollback, sanitized fixture, benchmark, and independent review.
5. Trial command guard, Google/NVIDIA methods, CrewAI teams, media, and publishing tools one at a time.

Completion gate: a provider-disabled architecture-factory fixture passes deterministic checks and recovery, and no candidate becomes default without a measured advantage and no critical safety regression.

### E4 - Bounded RAG And Obsidian / Graphify / Nexus

Objective: make retrieval and graph navigation source-grounded, efficient, explicit, and subordinate to the canonical knowledge layer.

Task families:

1. Preserve lexical retrieval as fallback and run the 20-query benchmark.
2. Require stable document/chunk IDs, metadata filters, citations, freshness, and source-boundary rejection.
3. Keep Graphify generated and write human conclusions to WikiLLM/Obsidian.
4. Separate Nexus config, Obsidian activation, socket, tool schema, and actual tool-call proof.
5. Trial Cognee, TurboVec, vector/hybrid retrieval, NVIDIA RAG/Retriever, and compression only after baseline evidence exists.
6. Curate LLM/agent research as a routing index and bounded synthesis, not a bulk paper dump.

Completion gate: the benchmark passes with no provenance or safety regression; all active vault/runtime claims have current evidence; any new retrieval layer remains rebuildable and non-canonical.

### E5 - Market-To-Payment Execution Packs

Objective: convert bounded market evidence into reviewed ICP, offer, content/outreach, buyer interaction, proposal, and payment-verdict packs.

Task families:

1. Validate the narrow cohort, anti-ICP, forcing moments, and account evidence with two independent signals.
2. Run primary buyer interviews and verify Product versus Security/Sales Engineering/RevOps ownership, alternatives, source-packet willingness, and trust constraints.
3. Produce a Product Knowledge Reliability Sprint for enterprise readiness, onboarding, and departure continuity; keep AI-agent context experimental and PRD as a downstream output.
4. Produce forcing-moment messaging, content, channel, and outreach packs from the reviewed audience knowledge node.
5. Define funnel, qualification, modeled-versus-observed ROI, pricing, proposal, access, and paid-start evidence.
6. Keep sending, private-channel access, publication, automated follow-up, pricing, terms, and payment actions owner-gated.
7. Separate attention, discovery, sales, proposal, paid intent, and payment evidence.

Completion gate: payment or firm approved paid start exists, or an evidence-based stop/revision verdict is accepted. The current desk-research package alone does not meet this gate.

### E6 - Dashboard, Editor, And Documentation

Objective: make architecture state understandable and editable without creating a shadow source of truth.

Task families:

1. Define canonical artifact schemas and proof/planning labels.
2. Maintain the three-block public surface: seven-layer tower, two-lane service pack, and disclosed knowledge/agent ROI scenario.
3. Maintain the documentation-first dashboard and parameter editor for architecture, knowledge, roles, skills, runs, evidence, and workflow state.
4. Keep Jarvis on a dedicated public UI with owner-gated API actions, server-side model allowlists, and writeback disabled. Provider execution remains blocked until atomic single-use approval and durable spend enforcement exist.
5. Produce safe review/export packets instead of direct writeback by default.
6. Preserve accessible motion, static/reduced-motion fallbacks, and mobile Stage views.
7. Route future video, LTX-2, Omma, Three.js, and similar tools through a media role and performance budget.
8. Require browser, accessibility, performance, safety, deploy, and Figma gates before production promotion.

Current evidence state: the local/public website, dashboard, and provider-disabled Jarvis contract have implementation and browser-QA proof. Production promotion, fresh deployment proof, live owner-authenticated provider execution, durable spend enforcement, and writeback remain separate gates.

Completion gate: the operator surface passes current browser/accessibility/performance/safety checks, every runtime state is truthful, and any production promotion receives explicit deploy and Figma approval.

### E7 - Safety, Observability, And Benchmarking

Objective: prove the architecture's quality, reliability, context efficiency, retrieval, memory, parallel coordination, cost, safety, and recovery.

Task families:

1. Run the paired baseline/candidate benchmark with identical inputs and budgets.
2. Record task outcomes, reviewer results, retry/loop state, tokens/context, latency/cost, retrieval, memory, conflicts, safety findings, and human effort.
3. Maintain trace, model-call, external-action, approval, and rollback ledgers without storing secrets or private raw content.
4. Run destructive, source-boundary, secret/path, unauthorized-write, self-approval, pause/resume, and recovery fixtures.
5. Promote only architectures and tools that pass the declared graduation gates.

Public lineage contracts are E7.1 unified observability ledger, E7.2 source-access threat/approval/recovery matrix, E7.3 cross-workstream benchmark and truth gate, and E7.4 the installability/MCP/admin/writeback safety gate. Exact dependencies and proof gates are in the public task-contract index.

Completion gate: no critical safety regression, no provenance regression, recovery and rollback demonstrated, and a meaningful quality/reliability or cost/latency benefit measured for the declared workload.

### E8 - Installable Knowledge Continuity Product

Objective: package the validated service architecture as a locally installable, permissioned knowledge-continuity administration system.

Task families:

1. Pass the E8.0 productization decision gate using E5 buyer/access/paid-start evidence and E7 safety evidence.
2. Build a GitHub-installable package or CLI for bounded discovery and reviewed export.
3. Build a least-privilege MCP server with read-only defaults and approval-gated write schemas.
4. Build an admin control plane for sources, goals, roles, permissions, evidence, approvals, drift, and audit receipts.
5. Prove install, upgrade, uninstall, rollback, documentation, sandbox isolation, and secret handling.
6. Run a guarded pilot and E7 benchmark before release.

Completion gate: sandbox install/uninstall and rollback pass; secrets are not stored; sources are cited; writeback requires human approval; tenant boundaries and audit receipts pass; and the buyer/access/paid-start evidence justifies productization.
