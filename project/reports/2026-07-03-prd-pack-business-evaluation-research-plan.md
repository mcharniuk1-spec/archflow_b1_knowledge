# PRD Pack Business Evaluation Research Plan

Date: 2026-07-03
Status: plan complete, research not executed
Scope: Product Discovery-to-Production PRD Pack as service, productized service, and future product

## Executive Decision

The next business-plan report should evaluate ArchFlow as a service first, productized service second, and software product third. Current evidence proves the internal method and the provider-disabled operating surface; it does not yet prove demand, willingness to pay, or a repeatable cloud product.

The research plan therefore asks one main question: can the PRD Pack become a paid wedge for B2B SaaS product teams, and which delivery model should be tested first?

## Target Audience Hypotheses

Primary ICP remains B2B SaaS product teams, Series B-D, 50-500 employees, 2-5 PMs, with Director or VP Product accountable for PRD quality and speed.

| Audience | Current confidence | What to prove |
|---|---|---|
| Director/VP Product in B2B SaaS | Medium as ICP hypothesis | Two independent public signals per target showing PRD, discovery, handoff, or team-scaling pain |
| Product Ops lead | Low-medium | Whether budget/ownership sits in Product Ops at larger accounts |
| Founder-led B2B startup | Later lane | Whether lack of PM team makes the offer simpler or too early |
| AI/product-service agency | Later lane | Whether this is an enablement product rather than first buyer |
| Enterprise product org | Later lane | Security/procurement barriers before value proof |

## Delivery-Model Evaluation

## Acceptance Criteria For The Deep Research

- Uses only approved sources or sanitized summaries.
- Separates FACT, INTERPRETATION, HYPOTHESIS, and GAP.
- Recommends first delivery model without claiming demand.
- Includes competitor matrix, audience map, pricing hypotheses, risk table, and execution roadmap.
- Produces a Notion-ready summary, PDF, Git commit, and Telegram packet after review.
- Leaves owner questions for E2 before real account research starts.
