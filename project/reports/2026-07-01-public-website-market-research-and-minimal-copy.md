## Competitor Pattern

FACT: Competitors converge on customer-signal capture, AI synthesis, prioritization, PRD/spec generation, knowledge assistants, and governance/integrations.

FACT: Productboard, Aha!, Dovetail, Zeda, BuildBetter, Fibery, Cycle, ChatPRD, Notion AI, Glean, and Dust already occupy pieces of the product-work, knowledge, insight, and agent-assistant market.

INTERPRETATION: ArchFlow should not present itself as a full Productboard, Aha!, Dovetail, Notion, Glean, or generic PRD-tool replacement.

HYPOTHESIS: The clean wedge is a service-led operating layer that works around the buyer's existing tools and turns scattered product material into reviewed delivery artifacts.

## Buyer Pain

- Product and customer context is scattered across calls, chats, docs, tickets, research, CRM, and roadmap tools.
- PRDs are rewritten because assumptions, evidence, tradeoffs, and ownership are unclear.
- Engineering handoff loses the reason behind the decision, not just the requirement text.
- Generic AI drafts are not trusted when source labels, permissions, review gates, and writeback boundaries are unclear.
- Heavy platforms can be too much when the immediate need is one reviewed PRD, ICP evidence packet, and task handoff.

## ICP

Primary ICP: B2B SaaS scaleups, roughly 50-500 employees, 2-5 PMs, and a Director or VP Product accountable for PRD quality, discovery-to-delivery speed, and cross-functional alignment.

Switching trigger: the team already has fragments in Notion, Jira, Linear, Productboard, Dovetail, docs, chats, or calls, but still lacks a reviewed, source-backed PRD and delivery handoff.

Secondary future audiences: Product Ops, founder-led B2B SaaS, customer-success/VoC leaders, RevOps/product-marketing teams, and enterprise AI transformation teams. These remain secondary for the current public landing.

## Public Website Copy Constraint

The landing should stay at three blocks:

1. Hero: source-backed PRDs for product teams with scattered context.
2. System: three tangible service outputs, not a feature grid.
3. Proof/trust: method and boundaries, including E1-E7 proof gates and no autonomous-writeback claim.

Preferred CTA: `Run workflow diagnostic`.

Trust line: source labels, explicit gaps, human approval, no autonomous writeback.

## Guardrails

- Do not claim guaranteed ROI.
- Do not claim validated demand before E5/E6/E7 proof.
- Do not claim provider-backed Jarvis.
- Do not claim Railway runtime.
- Do not claim browser-side provider calls.
- Do not claim autonomous writeback.
- Do not claim ArchFlow replaces full roadmap, feedback, or enterprise knowledge platforms.
